COMPLETE FINNISH NUMBER FORMATTING SPECIFICATION

This guide covers ALL number formatting for Finnish educational context.
Follow these rules strictly to ensure consistency across all questions.

=== BASIC RULES (Already Established) ===

1. DECIMAL SEPARATOR: ALWAYS comma (,)
   ✅ "15,25"  "0,5"  "3,14159"  "99,9"
   ❌ "15.25"  "0.5"  "3.14159" (English/international format)

2. LIST SEPARATOR: Semicolon + space (; )
   ✅ "5; 10; 15; 20"  "1,5; 2,3; 4,7"
   ❌ "5, 10, 15, 20" (ambiguous with decimal comma)
   ❌ "5;10;15;20" (no spaces, hard to read)

=== THOUSANDS SEPARATOR ===

3. THOUSANDS: Narrow non-breaking space (U+202F) OR regular space

   For numbers >= 10 000: Use space separator
   ✅ "10 000"  "1 000 000"  "15 250,50"
   ❌ "10000" (hard to read)
   ❌ "10,000" (English format, conflicts with decimal comma)
   ❌ "10.000" (German format, conflicts with decimal comma)

   For numbers 1000-9999: Space is optional but recommended
   ✅ "5 000" (recommended)
   ✅ "5000" (acceptable)
   ❌ "5,000" (never use comma)

   Group digits in threes from right to left:
   ✅ "1 234 567,89" (groups: 1|234|567,89)
   ❌ "12 34 567,89" (wrong grouping)

   Educational context: Use space for clarity
   ✅ "Väestö: 5 500 000" (easy to read)
   ✅ "Laske: 1 000 + 2 500" (clear)

=== NEGATIVE NUMBERS ===

4. NEGATIVE SIGN: Use minus sign (−, U+2212) OR hyphen-minus (-)

   Preferred: Minus sign (−)
   ✅ "−5"  "−15,25"  "−1 000"

   Acceptable: Hyphen-minus (-)
   ✅ "-5"  "-15,25"  "-1000"

   Always attach to number (no space):
   ✅ "−5"
   ❌ "− 5" (space between)

   In ranges or subtraction, use minus with spaces:
   ✅ "5 − 3 = 2" (subtraction)
   ✅ "lämpötila −5 °C" (temperature)
   ❌ "5−3=2" (no spaces, hard to read)

=== FRACTIONS ===

5. FRACTIONS: Inline slash (/) OR Unicode fractions

   Simple fractions: Use Unicode if available
   ✅ "½"  "¼"  "¾"  (preferred for common fractions)
   ✅ "1/2"  "1/4"  "3/4" (acceptable alternative)

   Complex fractions: Use slash
   ✅ "3/8"  "5/16"  "7/12"
   ❌ "3 / 8" (no spaces around slash in fractions)

   Mixed numbers: Space between whole and fraction
   ✅ "2 ½" or "2 1/2"
   ❌ "2½" (no space, hard to read)

   Text form (when emphasizing reading):
   ✅ "yksi kahdesosa"  "kolme neljäsosaa"

   Decimal form often preferred in answers:
   ✅ "0,5" (instead of ½) for calculations
   ✅ "0,75" (instead of ¾) for measurements

=== UNITS AND SPACING ===

6. SI UNITS: Always space between number and unit

   Length, mass, volume:
   ✅ "15 cm"  "20 kg"  "500 ml"  "1 000 m"
   ❌ "15cm"  "20kg"  "500ml" (no space)

   Area and volume units:
   ✅ "25 m²"  "15 cm²"  "1 000 cm³"
   ❌ "25m²"  "15cm²" (no space)

   Time units:
   ✅ "10 s"  "5 min"  "2 h"
   ❌ "10s"  "5min"  "2h"

   Special case - degrees of angle:
   ✅ "45°"  "90°"  "180°" (no space before degree symbol)
   ❌ "45 °" (space not used for angles)

=== TEMPERATURE ===

7. TEMPERATURE: Space before degree symbol + unit

   Celsius (standard in Finland):
   ✅ "20 °C"  "100 °C"  "−5 °C"
   ❌ "20°C"  "20° C"  "20 C" (missing space or symbol)

   Kelvin (used in physics):
   ✅ "273 K" (no degree symbol for Kelvin)
   ❌ "273°K" (incorrect)

   In ranges:
   ✅ "−10 °C to 25 °C"
   ✅ "lämpötila-alue −10...25 °C"

=== PERCENTAGES ===

8. PERCENTAGES: NO space before percent sign

   Standard format:
   ✅ "15%"  "99,5%"  "0,25%"  "100%"
   ❌ "15 %"  "99,5 %" (space not used in Finnish)

   In sentences:
   ✅ "Osuus on 25% kokonaismäärästä"
   ✅ "Kasvoi 5,5 prosenttia" (written out)

=== SCIENTIFIC NOTATION ===

9. SCIENTIFIC NOTATION: Use multiplication sign (×) and superscript

   Preferred format:
   ✅ "1,5 × 10³"  "6,022 × 10²³"  "3 × 10⁸"

   Alternative (middle dot):
   ✅ "1,5 · 10³" (acceptable)

   Avoid E-notation in educational context:
   ❌ "1,5E3"  "1.5e23" (programming notation, not educational)

   Large numbers: Scientific notation for clarity
   ✅ "Valonopeus: 3 × 10⁸ m/s"
   ✅ "Avogadron luku: 6,022 × 10²³"

   Superscripts to use:
   ⁰ ¹ ² ³ ⁴ ⁵ ⁶ ⁷ ⁸ ⁹ ⁺ ⁻

=== RANGES AND INTERVALS ===

10. RANGES: Use en-dash (–, U+2013) with spaces OR ellipsis (...)

    Standard range (recommended):
    ✅ "5–10"  "1–100"  "−5–15"
    ✅ "5 – 10" (with spaces, more readable)

    Avoid hyphen for ranges:
    ❌ "5-10" (ambiguous, looks like subtraction)

    Ellipsis notation (common in Finnish):
    ✅ "5...10"  "1...100"  "−5...25"

    With units:
    ✅ "10–15 cm"  "20–25 kg"
    ✅ "10...15 cm" (ellipsis alternative)

    Temperature ranges:
    ✅ "−10–25 °C"
    ✅ "−10...25 °C"

=== COORDINATES AND POINTS ===

11. COORDINATES: Use parentheses with semicolon separator

    2D coordinates:
    ✅ "(5; 3)"  "(−2; 7)"  "(0; 0)"
    ❌ "(5, 3)" (comma conflicts with decimal separator)

    3D coordinates:
    ✅ "(5; 3; 8)"  "(−1; 0; 4)"

    On number line / graph:
    ✅ "piste A = (5; 3)"
    ✅ "origo = (0; 0)"

=== CURRENCY (EUROS) ===

12. CURRENCY: Number + space + € symbol

    Standard format:
    ✅ "15 €"  "99,50 €"  "1 000 €"
    ❌ "€15"  "15€"  "EUR 15" (not standard in Finnish context)

    In sentences:
    ✅ "Hinta on 25,50 €"
    ✅ "Maksoi 1 500 euroa" (written out)

    Cents alone:
    ✅ "50 c" (rare, usually use 0,50 €)

=== MATHEMATICAL OPERATORS ===

13. OPERATORS: Space around operators for readability

    Basic arithmetic:
    ✅ "5 + 3"  "10 − 4"  "6 × 7"  "20 ÷ 5"
    ❌ "5+3"  "10-4"  "6×7" (no spaces, cramped)

    Equals sign:
    ✅ "5 + 3 = 8"
    ❌ "5+3=8" (no spaces)

    Multiplication signs:
    ✅ "5 × 3" (cross, preferred)
    ✅ "5 · 3" (middle dot, acceptable)
    ❌ "5 * 3" (asterisk, programming notation)

    Division:
    ✅ "20 ÷ 5" (division sign)
    ✅ "20 / 5" (slash, acceptable)
    ✅ "20:5" (colon, acceptable in Finnish)

    Inline fractions vs. division:
    ✅ "1/2" (fraction, no spaces)
    ✅ "10 / 5" (division, with spaces)

=== EXPONENTS AND POWERS ===

14. EXPONENTS: Use superscript numbers

    Powers:
    ✅ "2³"  "10⁶"  "x²"  "5⁴"
    ❌ "2^3"  "10^6" (programming notation)

    Area and volume:
    ✅ "m²"  "cm³"  "km²"
    ❌ "m2"  "cm3" (not superscript)

    Special: Square root
    ✅ "√16 = 4"
    ✅ "²√16 = 4" (if root index needed)

=== TIME FORMAT ===

15. TIME: Use colon (:) separator, 24-hour format

    Hours and minutes:
    ✅ "14:30"  "09:15"  "23:45"
    ❌ "14.30"  "2:30 PM" (not Finnish format)

    With seconds:
    ✅ "14:30:45"

    Duration:
    ✅ "2 h 30 min"  "1 h 15 min"
    ✅ "2,5 h" (decimal hours)

=== ANGLES ===

16. ANGLES: Degree symbol without space

    Degrees:
    ✅ "45°"  "90°"  "180°"  "360°"
    ❌ "45 °" (no space for angles, unlike temperature)

    With direction:
    ✅ "kulma α = 45°"
    ✅ "käännös 90° myötäpäivään"

=== PHONE NUMBERS (if needed) ===

17. PHONE NUMBERS: Group with spaces

    Finnish mobile:
    ✅ "040 123 4567"
    ❌ "0401234567" (hard to read)

    International:
    ✅ "+358 40 123 4567"

=== ABBREVIATIONS ===

18. LARGE NUMBER ABBREVIATIONS (optional, use carefully)

    In informal context:
    ✅ "5 milj." (5 miljoonaa)
    ✅ "2 mrd." (2 miljardia)

    Better: Write out or use scientific notation
    ✅ "5 000 000" or "5 × 10⁶"

=== SUMMARY QUICK REFERENCE ===

| Context | Format | Example | Wrong Example |
|---------|--------|---------|---------------|
| Decimal | comma | "15,25" | "15.25" |
| Thousands | space | "1 000" | "1,000" |
| List | semicolon+space | "5; 10; 15" | "5, 10, 15" |
| Negative | minus/hyphen | "−5" or "-5" | "- 5" |
| Fraction | slash or Unicode | "½" or "1/2" | "1 / 2" |
| Unit | space | "15 cm" | "15cm" |
| Temperature | space + °C | "20 °C" | "20°C" |
| Angle | no space + ° | "45°" | "45 °" |
| Percentage | no space + % | "15%" | "15 %" |
| Range | en-dash/ellipsis | "5–10" or "5...10" | "5-10" |
| Coordinates | parentheses+semicolon | "(5; 3)" | "(5, 3)" |
| Currency | number+space+€ | "15 €" | "€15" |
| Operators | spaces | "5 + 3 = 8" | "5+3=8" |
| Time | colon | "14:30" | "14.30" |
| Scientific | × and superscript | "1,5 × 10³" | "1.5E3" |

✅ "Vesi jäätyy 0 °C:ssa ja kiehuu 100 °C:ssa."

✅ "Järjestä luvut suuruusjärjestykseen: 2,71; 2,70; 2,17; 3,14"

=== SPECIAL EDUCATIONAL CONSIDERATIONS ===

1. CONSISTENCY: Use same format throughout a question set
   - Don't mix "1 000" and "1000" in same set
   - Choose en-dash OR ellipsis for ranges, use consistently

2. READABILITY: Prioritize clarity for students
   - Use spaces in large numbers (easier to read)
   - Use × for multiplication (clearer than ·)
   - Use full degree symbol (°C not just C)

3. AGE-APPROPRIATE: Simplify for younger grades
   - Grade 4: Avoid scientific notation, use "5 000 000"
   - Grade 5-6: Can introduce "5 × 10⁶"

4. FINNISH CONTEXT: Follow Finnish conventions
   - Comma for decimals (international may use period)
   - Space + € (not €15 or EUR)
   - 24-hour clock (not AM/PM)

5. AVOID AMBIGUITY: When in doubt, add clarity
   - Use "20 °C" not "20° C" or "20C"
   - Use "(5; 3)" not "(5,3)" for coordinates
   - Use "5...10" not "5-10" for ranges
